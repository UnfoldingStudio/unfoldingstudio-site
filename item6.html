Scienza e Fede, illustration by Beppe Giacobbe

This illustration made me think of a reflection by Antonino Zichichi in his book “Faith, Science and Technology”, where the dialogue between science and faith is presented as a shared search for truth:
“We are the proof of what emerges from the symbiosis between the two existential spheres: Immanent and Transcendent".

"Mankind can never be described by an equation, as they are a reality that is not limited to the immanent properties of their existence. When in five billion years the Sun goes out, the transcendental sphere of our existence will not be disturbed by this cosmic event.”

“«Science has its roots in the Immanent but leads mankind toward the Transcendent».

This quotation is a truth that arises from the fact that it is by studying ‘common matter,’ such as stones, that Science discovers fundamental truths. According to pre-Galilean thought, common matter was not supposed to be the bearer of any of these truths. That which leads to the Transcendent is proven by the fact that Science discovers a rigorous and immutable Logic at the foundation of all that exists: from the smallest element of the subnuclear world to the edges of the Cosmos.”
Could all of this have come from chaos?

Antonino Zichichi (born in Trapani in 1929) is an Italian physicist and Professor Emeritus at the University of Bologna. He contributed to nuclear and subnuclear physics, including the discovery of the antideuteron and precise studies of the weak interaction and the muon’s anomalous magnetic moment. He founded the Ettore Majorana Centre for Scientific Culture in Erice and served as president of the National Institute for Nuclear Physics and the European Physical Society.
